Old Parliament House, Sofia

The National Assembly Building is used by the Bulgarian parliament for parliamentary debates.
The main building has been proclaimed a monument of culture for its historic significance.
Situated in downtown Sofia, it was designed in Neo-Renaissance style by Konstantin Jovanoviƒá, a Serbian-Bulgarian architect
who received his education in Vienna and Switzerland and whose other works include the Parliament of Serbia building.
It was constructed between 1884 and 1886 by Friederich Wilhelm Gustav Liebe, a young builder from Saxony who was 22 years
old when construction began.
The building was originally painted in an off-white hue, but since the latter part of the 20th century has been white.
The building is depicted on the reverse of the Bulgarian 20 leva banknote, issued in 1999 and 2007.
